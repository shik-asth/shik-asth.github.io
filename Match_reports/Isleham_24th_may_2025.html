The Ripple Effect

Fen Ditton CC vs Isleham CC, 24th May 2025

Scorecard

Gustave Le Bon coined the term "behavioral contagion" in 18951, referring to the phenomenon where individuals in a group unconsciously mimic the actions of others, a ripple effect, if you will. Generally what is collectively perceived as a good (or bad) idea, is magnified by the rest of the group. Sitting on the sidelines scoring, as we’d chosen to bat first, I witnessed this unfold in real time. One by one, players started chipping in with suggestions for the match report providing brilliant insights, and hysterical takes on the game2. It made my job so easy that I confidently declared the match report had already been written, before the match had even ended. We will conveniently ignore the fact that it still took me over ten days to actually publish it, prompting Paul to ask, with his trademark dry delivery, “I thought the match report was already written?”

The match was played at Isleham, a ground that seemed to share space with what I assume was the town’s central auditorium. It was lovely to see the locals out in force, enjoying the sun, the cricket, and the beer on tap. The ground featured two large sightscreens, which inspired Arko to bet that Sameer would insist on adjusting them. I thought they were fine and took the bet. Naturally, I lost. Sameer moved them three times. The aforementioned beer was promptly enjoyed by Adam R., who was immediately chastised by a few players. Personally, I disagreed. One pint in, Adam transforms into peak commentator mode. We need more of that.

Arko and Jonny3 opened the batting, and just as swiftly closed it. Arko was given LBW to a ball that hit him somewhere near the crotch, and he seemed quite willing to strip and provide evidence to the team. A possibilty of a charge of indecent exposure is probably why he did not go through with the show. As soon as the decision was given, Paul executed a table-smash that would’ve made the Hulk pause. I’ve genuinely never seen him that angry4, and he immediately went for a calming walk. This moment also gave rise to the “brace is an exoskeleton” jokes, courtesy of Jack, Bethan, and Adam R. While AB and Sameer rebuilt the innings, the mood on the sidelines turned... inclusive. Jokes flew, and we managed to tick off every major category of political incorrectness. In the middle of a fat joke, Adam R. broke a chair, claiming it was the burden of being a “thiccc boi.” On the field, debutant umpire Chetan added flair by dabbing when signalling wides. Back to the cricket: AB played a classy knock of 75 off 88, James smacked a rapid 52 off 35, and the rest chipped in to push us to a strong total of 243. Isleham’s bowlers were led by Solomon, with 2 for 35, while Luke, Jamie, Wade, and Jack each took one wicket.

After a top-tier tea, Fen Ditton returned to doing what we’re best known for: bowling. If you expected me to say “fielding,” I recommend speaking to a professional. Adam R., showing immense bravery (or perhaps poor judgment), donned the keeper’s pads but skipped the rest of the protective gear. By the end of the innings, he’d been struck on just about every body part available. The second innings was fairly uneventful. With a big total on the board, our bowlers chipped away steadily. There was a brief wobble during a 54-run partnership between Wade, who batted through for a solid 55* off 97, and Jack, who contributed 24 off 21. Sameer broke the stand, and we were back in control. Wickets were shared: James, AB, and Arko took two each; Sameer, Manu, and I claimed one apiece. Special mention to our fielders, who held onto five catches this time (Jack, Sameer, Arko, and Jonny). That alone might qualify as victory. A comfortable win in the end.
